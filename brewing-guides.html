Preparation

The Perfect Pour

Begin with pure, filtered spring water. The kinetic energy of the pour awakens the leaves, initiating the delicate unrolling process essential for extraction.

We recommend using a gooseneck kettle to control the flow and gracefully saturate every leaf, laying the foundation for a flawless cup.

Heat

Temperature Control

Green teas require a gentle 175°F to prevent bitterness, while bold black teas demand a rolling boil. Precision is the key to unlocking true flavor.

Using water that is too hot can scorch delicate leaves, destroying the nuanced aromatic oils that took months or even years to develop.

Infusion

Time & Patience

Steeping is a waiting game. Adhere strictly to the recommended seconds. Over-steeping releases tannins that overpower the subtle floral and earthy notes.

Watch as the leaves perform the 'agony of the leaves'—a beautiful rhythmic dance as they absorb water and unfurl gracefully before your eyes.

Experience

Mindful Consumption

Do not rush. Inhale the aroma deeply before the first sip. Let the liquid coat your palate, and experience the journey from the first note to the lingering finish.

Tea is an invitation to pause. Turn this simple act of consumption into a meditative, sensory ritual that centers your mind and spirit.
